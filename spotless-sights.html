The Process

I created this site in the beginning of 2024 for a local business. The first design was created just using CSS and HTML with no Adobe XD wireframing or prototyping. I also created the logo for the company using Canva.


Towards the end of 2024, I wanted to redesign the site. Some critiques I had for my initial design were the abundance of white space, the inconsistency in the color palette for the footer, and the bottom gallery was not intuitive. The bottom gallery was initially a javascript transformation that would show the dirty window then fade into the clean window, but I didn't feel that all users would grasp what was happening.


I also decided to redesign the company logo, hoping to create a more warm and inviting feeling. The current logo seemed a little too sharp and the icon was not as visible when printing.

These were 2 initial redesign ideas I crafted for the site. The end product became a mix of these two with the mobile design following that of the left image and the web design following that of the right image.

These are the final redesigned logos! They wanted a logo which would be fit for printing on hats and usable for circular designs which is the top illustration. They also wanted a design that would be fit for t-shirts and general advertisements, the second logo image!

To the left is the final product in the web view! I think the addition of borders, color blocks, and images into the site made it feel less empty and serve as a guide to the eye of the user as they traverse the site. I also feel that the simplification of the gallery to show the before and after images simultaneously better serves this company's audience.
